Why it's different

Most "sourdough" isn't.

Commercial bakeries use baker's yeast with added flavoring to fake the sourdough taste — it's faster and cheaper. JustBread uses only wild yeast, a nine-day cold ferment, and three ingredients. That's the entire list.

The process

Nine days is the shortcut.

There is no faster way to make bread like this. Every stage serves a purpose.

01
Starter maintenance
A live wild yeast culture — no commercial yeast ever — is fed and maintained continuously. This is what makes the fermentation real.
02
Autolyse & mixing
Flour and water are combined and rested before the starter and salt are incorporated. This develops the gluten structure without overworking the dough.
03
Bulk fermentation — days 1–3
The dough ferments at cool temperatures with periodic stretch-and-fold sets. Flavor and structure develop slowly.
04
Cold proof — days 3–9
The shaped loaf goes into the refrigerator for a long, slow final proof. This is where the depth of flavor comes from — there is no substitute for time.
05
Baked, then shipped
Baked in a Dutch oven for maximum oven spring and crust development. Packaged and out the door the same day.
