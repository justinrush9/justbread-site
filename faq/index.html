Delivery & freshness
Local delivery: typically within hours of baking, same day.

Illinois shipping: loaves are baked Wednesday morning and ship the same day via UPS Ground. Most customers receive their loaf on Thursday — within 24 hours of it leaving the oven. You'll get a tracking number by email once it ships.
The long fermentation naturally extends shelf life — you'll get 4–5 days at room temperature in a paper bag or bread box, or up to a week if you keep it cut-side-down wrapped in a clean towel.

You can also freeze it. Slice before freezing, then toast straight from frozen — it comes back almost entirely.

The bread
Flour, water, salt. That's the entire ingredient list — no commercial yeast, no dough conditioners, no preservatives, no additives of any kind.
A long cold fermentation develops flavor that simply doesn't exist in a fast-fermented bread. The wild yeast works slowly at refrigerator temperatures, producing lactic and acetic acids that create complexity in the crumb and a crust with real depth.

Most commercial sourdough ferments for a few hours. Nine days isn't a marketing number — it's the actual process.
